Easy Ways to Apply for a Working Capital Loan in India

A working capital loan is a financial boost for your business, offering a quick infusion of cash to help manage short-term operational needs. It's designed to enhance your company's liquidity, ensuring smooth operation without dipping into long-term investments or assets. This loan is especially beneficial for bridging gaps in cash flow, financing daily operations, or tackling unexpected expenses.

Feature of Working Capital Loan

  • Loan Amount: Offers a wide range from ₹50,000 to ₹ 35 lakhs, catering to both small and large needs.
  • Fast Processing: Applications processed on average within 72 hours, facilitating swift financial assistance.
  • Online Processing: A fully digital application process eliminates the need for physical visits, saving time and effort.
  • No Collateral Required: An collateral free business loan option, minimizing risk to your business assets.
  • Competitive Interest Rates: Interest rates start from 13.5% annually, tailored according to your credit profile.
  • Flexible Loan Tenure: Loan tenure varies from 1 to 36 months, supporting easy and convenient repayment options.
  • Processing Fee: A one-time fee of 2-5%, with no hidden costs, ensuring transparency.
  • Flexible Repayments: Offers the flexibility of monthly or bi-weekly installments.

Comparative Analysis of Do's and Don'ts When Considering a Working Capital Loan

Aspect Do Don't
Evaluating Alternatives Assess other working capital management strategies like reducing expenses and optimizing invoices. Jump straight to applying for a loan without attempting to improve financial management through internal measures.
Strategic Utilization Have a detailed plan for the utilization of loan funds, specifically for short-term needs. Borrow without a specific plan, risking the funds on inappropriate expenses or long-term investments.
Assessing Business Benefits Consider the loan's benefits to your operation's efficiency and ensure ability to service the loan. Apply for a loan without evaluating your business's revenue adequacy for repayments, especially if the business is relatively new.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Business operational for more than 3 months.
  • Minimum turnover of ₹90,000 in the last 3 months.
  • Business not listed under the blacklisted/excluded category for SBA finance.
  • The business location should not be in a negative list area.
  • Trusts, NGOs, and charitable institutions are ineligible.

Documents Required

  • Proof of business operation for over 3 months.
  • Financial statements showing a turnover of ₹90,000 for the last 3 months.
  • Proof that the business is not blacklisted or excluded for SBA finance.
  • Verification of business location outside negative list areas.

Benefits of Working Capital Loan

  • Short-tenured Loan: With terms from 1 to 36 months, it's perfect for covering immediate needs without long-term debt.
  • Handle Financial Difficulties: Provides a cushion during cash flow shortages, allowing your business to continue operations smoothly.
  • No Collateral/Security Required for Unsecured Loans: Keeps your assets safe while you access the funds needed.
  • Helps in Lean Periods: Offers financial stability during seasonal sales fluctuations, ensuring business continuity.
  • Spend at Your Discretion: The funds can be used for any business need, offering complete flexibility in managing your operations.

Frequently Asked Questions:

+

How do I approach Lendingkart for a working capital loan?

The application process is completely online. You can create a free Lendingkart account here or use our mobile app for applying for a small business loan.

+

How much working capital finance can I avail?

You can avail working capital loans ranging from ₹50,000 to ₹2 Crore, depending on your business's financial health, including monthly sales turnover, business vintage, credit score, and industry type.

+

What can be accepted as security?

Lendingkart offers unsecured working capital loans, which means no physical collateral, property mortgage, or fixed deposit pledge is required. However, we may require a personal guarantee from the business owner.

+

How long will it take to get the loan disbursed?

The loan disbursement process typically takes 2-3 business days after complete application submission, document verification, loan approval, and agreement signing. In some cases, disbursement can happen within 24 hours if all documents are in order.

+

Why should I take your product if I have a CC or OD limit?

Our working capital loan offers several advantages over traditional CC/OD: no usage restrictions, flexible repayment options, no minimum balance requirements, competitive interest rates, and quick disbursement.

+

Can I get funds for long-term?

Yes, Lendingkart offers flexible tenure options: short-term (3-12 months), medium-term (1-2 years), and long-term (up to 5 years). The tenure is determined based on your business needs and repayment capacity.

+

Difference between term loan and working capital loan?

Term loans are for long-term investments with longer tenures (up to 10 years) and fixed EMIs, while working capital loans are for day-to-day operations with shorter tenures (up to 5 years) and flexible repayment options.

+

What is CIBIL Score?

CIBIL Score is a 3-digit numeric summary of your credit history (300-900) that reflects your credit repayment history, utilization, mix, and length of credit history. A higher score indicates better creditworthiness.

+

Will you check my CIBIL?

Yes, we check your CIBIL score as part of our loan evaluation process. However, we consider multiple factors, not just CIBIL. Business performance is equally important, and we may approve loans even with lower scores.

+

Why do you ask for PDC when we are making instalment payment through NACH/ECS/RTGS/NEFT?

PDC (Post Dated Cheques) serve as a backup payment mechanism, ensuring payment security, acting as a fallback option, helping in case of technical issues with electronic payments, and providing additional documentation.

+

What if instalments are paid late?

Late payments may result in late payment charges, affect your credit score, impact future loan eligibility, and lead to additional interest charges. We recommend setting up automatic payments to avoid late payments.

+

What is NACH Mandate?

NACH (National Automated Clearing House) Mandate is an electronic payment system that automates EMI payments, ensures timely payments, reduces manual intervention, and provides payment confirmation. It's a secure and convenient way to make regular payments.

+

Are there any foreclosure charges?

Lendingkart offers flexible foreclosure options with no foreclosure charges after 6 months and minimal charges before 6 months. This helps save on interest costs and improves your credit score.

+

Do you provide loans to service sector?

Yes, we provide working capital loans to various service sectors including IT and Technology, Healthcare, Education, Consulting, Professional Services, and many more.

+

Can I apply for a second loan before the repayment of the first loan is completed?

Yes, you can apply for a second loan if you have a good repayment history, your business shows growth, you meet the eligibility criteria, and your credit score is healthy. Each application is evaluated independently.
