What are MSME Loans?

An Micro Small Medium Enterprise loan, also known as a SME loan, is a type of credit facility given to micro, small, and medium-sized businesses by banks, small finance banks, and non-bank financing companies like Lendingkart. These loans are also available to startups. These loans can be used for a variety of things, such as purchasing inventory, investing in plant and machinery, and paying employees or suppliers.

Types of loans for enterprises of Micro, Small and Medium size

Here are the main types of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprise loans in India:

Term Loans

Term loans are a major category of MSME finance that provide funds for long-term capital expenditure. They can be used to purchase machinery, set up a new unit, expand an existing unit, or undertake other capital-intensive projects. Term loans are repaid in fixed installments over a predetermined period, and the interest rate may be fixed or floating. These loans are a great way to fund smaller or larger projects of a business. Since the repayment is made over a fixed term with a set repayment structure, during the initial repayment period the interest component is usually high compared to the principal component in the repayment, which switches the other way around as more and more repayments are done.

Flexi Loans

A Flexi loan is a type of personal loan that offers the flexibility to access funds whenever needed. With a pre-approved loan limit, you can borrow from this limit multiple times without having to reapply for a new loan each time.

Working Capital Loans

Working capital loans are one of the leading SME loans in India, these loans can help the entrepreneur navigate through daily business operations by injecting debt funds for the short term, so that entrepreneurs can better manage their business. Working capital loans provide funds to meet the day-to-day operational expenses of a business, such as raw material purchases, inventory management, salary payments, and other working capital requirements. These short-term loans help ensure smooth business operations by providing adequate cash flow. Working capital loans can be secured or unsecured, depending on the lender's requirements and the borrower's creditworthiness.

Overdraft Facilities

Number of MSMEs in India has shot up considerably over the last couple of years, naturally for business sustenance, these businesses often require a lot of capital to keep afloat. Overdraft solution is a type of MSME loan where businesses can withdraw more funds than the balance available in their current account. Overdraft solution provides flexibility for managing cash flow by allowing businesses to overdraw to a certain limit from their account. The limit is sanctioned by the bank or financial institution, against which they charge interest on the amount withdrawn.

Other MSME Loan Types

In addition to term loans and working capital loans, MSME businesses can also avail of other specialized loan types, such as:

  • Equipment/Machinery Loans: To purchase machinery or equipment,these loans are specifically designed to enable entrepreneurs to make the machinery purchases. This is essential for business operations and expansion.
  • Invoice Financing: Also known as factoring, where businesses can obtain funds by selling their outstanding invoices to a financial institution at discounted rates, once the invoices are purchased by the third party, the responsibility for collections of bills receivables gets transferred to the purchaser of the invoice. This is a great way for businesses to secure funding where there are pending bills receivables and the business cycles are longer.
  • Bank Guarantees: Where a bank or financial institution guarantees payment to a third party on behalf of the borrower, in case the borrower defaults, the bank becomes liable for making payment to the supplier who has supplied the material based on the bank guarantees.
  • Letters of Credit: Where a bank or financial institution issues a letter of credit on behalf of the borrower to guarantee payment to the beneficiary. These letters of credit act as bank guarantees to the suppliers on the behalf of the borrowers.

Government Schemes for MSME Loans

The Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE) is a key government scheme that provides collateral-free credit to MSMEs. Some key features of the CGTMSE scheme:

  • It covers collateral-free credit facilities (term loans and/or working capital) up to ₹100 lakh extended by banks and financial institutions to new and existing micro and small enterprises.
  • The guarantee cover provided is up to 75% of the credit facility up to ₹50 lakh (85% for loans up to ₹5 lakh to micro enterprises, 80% for MSEs owned/operated by women and all loans to the North-Eastern Region).
  • For credit facilities above ₹50 lakh and up to ₹100 lakh, the guarantee cover is 50% of the credit exposure.
  • Eligible lending institutions can apply to the CGTMSE office to avail the guarantee support, while MSMEs can directly approach banks and financial institutions for the collateral-free loans.

Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS)

The ECLGS was launched in 2020 as a special scheme to provide additional credit to businesses, including MSMEs, impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic. Key features of the ECLGS:

  • It provides 100% guaranteed emergency credit lines to eligible MSMEs and businesses to meet their operational liabilities and restart their businesses.
  • The scheme covers loans up to ₹4.5 crore for MSMEs and ₹200 crore for other eligible borrowers.
  • The loans have a tenure of 5 years, including a 1-year moratorium on principal repayment.
  • The interest rate on ECLGS loans is capped at 9.25% for banks and 14% for NBFCs.
  • The scheme is available to all MSME borrowers with loans up to ₹50 crore and turnover up to ₹250 crore as on 29th February 29, 2020.

Both the CGTMSE and ECLGS schemes have been instrumental in providing timely and collateral-free credit support to MSMEs, especially during the challenging times of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Applying for MSME Loans

Here are some common mistakes to avoid when applying for MSME loans:

Insufficient Documentation

One of the biggest mistakes businesses make is providing incomplete or inaccurate documentation. Lenders rely heavily on the information you provide to assess your creditworthiness. Failing to submit all required documents or providing inconsistent information can lead to delays or rejections. To avoid this, carefully review the lender's documentation requirements and ensure you provide all necessary paperwork. Be meticulous in your record-keeping and double-check all information before submission.

Neglecting Credit Score

Your business's creditworthiness is a critical factor in loan approval. Many SMEs fail to monitor and maintain their credit scores, which can negatively impact their loan applications. To prevent this, regularly check your business credit score, address any inaccuracies, and work on improving it over time. Paying bills on time and managing debts responsibly is key to maintaining a strong credit profile.It is imperative to continuously monitor your credit score to ensure that the lending terms remain favorable for the business. There are several reasons due to which the credit score may take a hit, factors such as delayed repayments, missed repayments, default on loans, are among a couple of many factors which may result in your credit score taking a hit. Being aware about your current score, helps you understand what practices can you follow to increase and improve credit records.

Over-borrowing

Another common mistake is borrowing more than your business can realistically repay. While securing a larger loan may seem tempting, it can lead to financial strain and difficulties in meeting repayment obligations. Before applying, carefully assess your business's financial needs and borrow only what you can comfortably pay back. Use online tools like EMI calculators to determine the monthly installments you can manage based on your cash flow.

Ignoring the Fine Print

Understanding the terms and conditions of your loan is crucial. Some businesses make the mistake of overlooking the fine print, which can result in unexpected costs, penalties, or unfavorable terms down the line. Always read and comprehend the loan agreement thoroughly, and if necessary, seek legal or financial advice to ensure you fully understand the terms.

Lack of a Solid Business Plan

A well-thought-out business plan is essential for demonstrating to lenders that you have a clear strategy for repaying the loan. Some applicants make the error of not developing a comprehensive plan or providing one that lacks detail and substance. To avoid this, invest time and effort in crafting a robust business plan that outlines your business's goals, financial projections, and repayment methods. By being mindful of these common pitfalls and taking proactive steps to address them, you can enhance your chances of securing an MSME loan that aligns with your business's needs and goals.
